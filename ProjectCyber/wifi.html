WI-FI SAFETY GUIDE

Public Wi-Fi is a goldmine for attackers. Learn what's at risk, how attacks work, and how to stay protected.

☕

Open Public Wi-Fi

Coffee shops, airports, hotels — no password required means no encryption. Anyone on the same network can potentially intercept your traffic.

😈

Evil Twin Networks

Attackers set up a fake hotspot named "Airport_Free_WiFi" near a real one. Your device connects automatically, sending all traffic through the attacker.

🏨

Shared Password Networks

Hotel or gym Wi-Fi with a shared password is slightly better — but all users share the same network. Advanced users can still sniff traffic.

🏠

Private Home Network

WPA3/WPA2 encryption with a strong unique password. You control who is on the network. Still, keep firmware updated and change default router passwords.

📱

Mobile Data (4G/5G)

Your carrier's encrypted cellular network is far more secure than public Wi-Fi. Use it for sensitive tasks when you don't have trusted Wi-Fi access.

🛡️

VPN on Any Network

A VPN encrypts all your traffic before it leaves your device, making public Wi-Fi attacks useless. Recommended for any non-home network.

STAY SAFE ON ANY NETWORK

  • 01
    Use a reputable VPNConsider Mullvad, ProtonVPN, or ExpressVPN for public networks. Free VPNs often sell your data. Your VPN provider sees your traffic, so choose one with a no-logs policy.
  • 02
    Verify the network name before connectingAlways ask staff for the exact Wi-Fi name. "CoffeShop_WiFi" next to "CoffeeShop_WiFi" could be an evil twin. Subtle typos are a giveaway.
  • 03
    Use HTTPS everywhereLook for the padlock icon. Modern browsers warn you about HTTP sites. The HTTPS extension or browser settings can force secure connections. Never log in on HTTP sites.
  • 04
    Disable auto-connect to known networksYour device can auto-join any network matching a saved name. An attacker naming their hotspot "Home" or "Starbucks" can trick your device into connecting. Turn off auto-join for public networks.
  • 05
    Turn off Wi-Fi when not in useYour device broadcasts saved network names when Wi-Fi is on, even when not connected. This enables passive tracking and probing attacks. Enable Wi-Fi only when needed.
  • 06
    Avoid sensitive activities on public Wi-FiIf you don't have a VPN, avoid online banking, email login, or shopping. Save these for your mobile data or trusted home network. No deal is worth a compromised bank account.
  • 07
    Keep your device firewall enabledYour OS firewall blocks incoming connection attempts. On public networks, also enable "Public Network" mode which restricts network discovery and sharing — this prevents other devices from seeing yours.
  • 08
    Check for HSTS and certificate warningsIf your browser shows a certificate error, do not bypass it. This can indicate a man-in-the-middle attack intercepting your connection. Treat any unexpected security warning as a red flag.
